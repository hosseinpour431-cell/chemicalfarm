Hazard Classification

Combustible liquid; may cause mild eye/skin irritation

1,3-Propanediol is generally considered low in acute toxicity and is widely used in polymer applications. Industrial handling should still follow good practice: avoid aerosol generation, ensure ventilation, and keep away from strong ignition sources at elevated temperatures. Spills can be slippery; proper housekeeping is important.

Chronic Health Effects

Low chronic toxicity profile (typical use conditions)

Under typical industrial exposure controls, chronic risks are generally low. Prolonged or repeated contact may still contribute to dryness/irritation in sensitive individuals. Apply standard PPE (gloves, goggles) and follow workplace exposure guidance.

Production Process & Capacity

1,3-Propanediol is produced via two main routes: bio-fermentation (sugars/glycerol) and petrochemical synthesis (acrolein/propylene oxide derivatives). Bio-based PDO is widely used for renewable PTT value chains.
1 Feed Preparation
A renewable feed (e.g., sugar solution or refined glycerol) is conditioned and sterilized, then fed to the bioreactor under controlled pH and temperature.
2 Bioconversion
Microorganisms convert the feed to PDO with process controls to optimize yield, suppress by-products, and manage fermentation heat and oxygen demand.
3 Separation & Purification
The broth is clarified; PDO is recovered via a combination of filtration, evaporation, and distillation/ion-exchange (route-dependent) to reach polymer-grade specifications.
4 Finishing & Storage
Final polishing ensures low water and impurity levels for polyester/polyol applications. PDO is stored in suitable tanks (hygroscopic liquid) with moisture control.
