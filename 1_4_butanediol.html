Hazard Classification

Irritant; harmful if swallowed or inhaled

1,4-Butanediol is an irritant to skin, eyes, and respiratory system. It can cause drowsiness or dizziness if inhaled. Prolonged or repeated exposure can cause central nervous system effects. Keep away from heat, sparks, open flames and hot surfaces.

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated exposure effects

Repeated skin contact may cause irritation and defatting of the skin. Prolonged or repeated inhalation of vapors may cause respiratory irritation and central nervous system effects including headache, dizziness, and fatigue. Chronic exposure may affect liver and kidney function.

Production Process & Capacity

1,4-Butanediol is produced via multiple routes: acetylene (Reppe) process, maleic anhydride, and butadiene oxidation. Regional economics and feedstock availability drive process selection.
1 Feedstock Preparation
High-purity acetylene is prepared with formaldehyde and a copper-based catalyst system in a controlled aqueous environment for selective synthesis of 1,4-butanediol intermediates.
2 Catalytic Reaction
The Reppe reaction occurs under pressure (20-30 bar) at moderate temperatures (80-120°C): acetylene + 2 formaldehyde → 1,4-butynediol, followed by hydrogenation to 1,4-butanediol using nickel or palladium catalysts.
3 Product Separation
The crude product mixture undergoes multiple separation steps including filtration, extraction, and distillation to isolate pure 1,4-butanediol from reaction by-products and unreacted materials.
4 Purification & Storage
Final purification via vacuum distillation and crystallization to achieve polymer-grade purity. Stored in stainless steel tanks under nitrogen blanket to prevent oxidation and moisture absorption.

Global Demand Analysis

Asia-Pacific is the largest market for 1,4-butanediol driven by strong demand in polyurethane elastomers, PBT engineering plastics, and GBL applications. China alone accounts for over 50% of global consumption. North America and Europe show steady growth in high-performance polymers and coatings. The market is characterized by tight integration with THF and PTMEG production.

Market Context
1,4-Butanediol demand is primarily driven by high-performance polymers (PBT, polyurethanes), with significant growth in thermoplastic elastomers and electronic chemicals. The market is evolving toward greater integration with PTMEG and spandex value chains.
