Hazard Classification

Flammable; potential chronic toxicity concern (EHS-restricted solvent)

1,4-Dioxane is a highly water-miscible ether solvent with strict EHS attention in many jurisdictions due to chronic hazard concerns. It is flammable, and vapors can form ignitable mixtures. Use closed handling, local exhaust ventilation, and avoid releases to water systems. Substitute where possible and follow site-specific regulatory requirements.

Chronic Health Effects

Long-term exposure concerns; minimize occupational exposure

Chronic exposure is a key concern for 1,4-dioxane in many safety frameworks, so conservative exposure control is recommended: closed transfer, monitoring, and PPE (chemical goggles, suitable gloves). Avoid repeated inhalation exposure and ensure spill response prevents environmental release.

Production Process & Capacity

1,4-Dioxane is typically produced as a by-product/co-product from ethoxylation processes, or via controlled dehydration/condensation routes, followed by purification. Due to EHS constraints, many users prioritize substitution and closed-loop handling.
1 Formation / Generation
In ethoxylation units, side reactions can generate dioxane in the reaction mixture; formation level depends on operating conditions and catalyst/chemistry.
2 Separation
Crude streams are routed to separation where volatile components are recovered; dioxane may be isolated via distillation given its boiling point near 101–102°C.
3 Purification & Quality Control
Further purification removes water and trace organics. Because it is water-miscible, careful drying and specification control are required for solvent-grade use.
4 Closed Handling & Storage
Stored and transferred in closed systems with vapor control and secondary containment to minimize worker exposure and prevent environmental release.
