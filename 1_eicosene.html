Hazard Classification

Combustible/flammable hydrocarbon; can generate irritating fumes on heating

1-Eicosene is a long-chain linear alpha-olefin. Keep away from heat/ignition sources and strong oxidizers. Use ventilation during handling and apply anti-static/grounding measures during transfers.

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated exposure may cause skin dryness; mists may irritate airways

Prolonged or repeated contact with hydrocarbon liquids can defat the skin and cause dermatitis. Aerosols/mists may irritate the respiratory tract; follow PPE and occupational exposure controls.

Production Process & Capacity

1-Eicosene is supplied mainly via ethylene oligomerization LAO complexes , where long-chain cuts (C18–C22+) are recovered by deep fractionation. Depending on configuration, it may also arise from cut blending and specialty purification to meet comonomer/specs.
1 Feedstock Preparation
Polymer-grade ethylene is purified and dried; catalyst/co-catalyst systems are prepared under inert conditions to protect activity.
2 Catalytic Oligomerization
Ethylene is converted into a distribution of linear alpha-olefins (C4–C30+). Conditions and catalyst choice influence the long-chain fraction yield.
3 Deep Fractionation
Distillation separates heavy cuts; 1-eicosene is recovered from long-chain windows with tight specification control and polishing steps if required.
4 Purification & Storage
Final polishing improves stability and removes trace impurities. Stored as a combustible hydrocarbon with heating/trace heat as needed, plus grounding and vapor control.
