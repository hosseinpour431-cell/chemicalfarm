Hazard Classification

Irritant; flammable liquid and vapor

1-Hexadecene is a flammable liquid that may cause skin and eye irritation. Vapors may cause drowsiness or dizziness. Prolonged exposure may cause skin dryness or cracking. Keep away from heat, sparks, open flames and hot surfaces.

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated exposure effects

Repeated skin contact may cause defatting of the skin leading to dermatitis. Prolonged or repeated inhalation of vapors may cause respiratory irritation. Chronic exposure may affect the central nervous system.

Production Process & Capacity

1-Hexadecene is primarily produced via oligomerization of ethylene and paraffin cracking. Advanced processes focus on selective production of C16 fraction with high linearity and terminal unsaturation.
1 Feedstock Preparation
High-purity ethylene feed is prepared with catalyst systems (typically nickel-based) and co-catalysts in a controlled environment for selective oligomerization to C10+ alpha olefins.
2 Catalytic Reaction
Oligomerization occurs at moderate temperatures (60-120°C) and pressures (20-60 bar). The SHOP process uses nickel catalysts with selective chain growth to produce a range of linear alpha olefins, with C16 fraction later separated.
3 Fractionation
The crude oligomer mixture undergoes distillation to separate different carbon number fractions. Advanced fractionation techniques isolate the C16 cut containing predominantly 1-hexadecene with high purity.
4 Purification & Storage
Final purification via vacuum distillation and stabilization (with inhibitors) to prevent polymerization. Stored under nitrogen blanket in carbon steel tanks with appropriate fire protection measures.

Global Demand Analysis

North America and Europe represent the largest markets for 1-hexadecene due to their advanced polymer production capabilities. Asia-Pacific is experiencing growth driven by expanding polyethylene capacity and increasing demand for high-performance surfactants. The market is characterized by tight integration with polyolefin production facilities.
