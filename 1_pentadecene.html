Hazard Classification

Combustible/flammable hydrocarbon; avoid oxidizers and ignition sources

1-Pentadecene is a linear alpha-olefin hydrocarbon. Handle with good ventilation, keep away from heat/sparks/open flames, and store away from strong oxidizers. Ground/bond containers during transfers to reduce static discharge risk.

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated skin contact may cause dermatitis; mists can irritate airways

Prolonged or repeated contact with hydrocarbon liquids can defat the skin and cause dryness/cracking. Aerosols may irritate the respiratory system; apply PPE (gloves, goggles) and minimize mist generation.

Production Process & Capacity

1-Pentadecene is typically supplied within linear alpha-olefin (LAO) slates from ethylene oligomerization , then recovered by fractionation of mid-to-heavy cuts. It is used as comonomer for LLDPE/HDPE and as feedstock for surfactants and long-chain alcohols.
1 Feedstock Preparation
Polymer-grade ethylene is purified and dried. Catalyst/co-catalyst systems are handled under inert conditions to protect activity.
2 Catalytic Oligomerization
Ethylene is converted into a distribution of linear alpha-olefins (C4–C30+). Process conditions control the cut distribution and co-product slate.
3 Fractionation
Distillation separates mid-to-heavy cuts; 1-pentadecene is recovered via targeted cut boundaries, with impurity control for polymer/spec-grade supply.
4 Purification & Storage
Final polishing may improve stability and remove trace impurities. Stored as a combustible hydrocarbon with grounding/anti-static measures during transfer.
