Hazard Classification

Combustible liquid; causes irritation (skin/eyes/respiratory)

2-Ethylhexanol is a higher alcohol used mainly as an intermediate for plasticizers and 2-ethylhexyl acrylate. It is a combustible liquid with moderate vapor pressure; mists/vapors can irritate eyes and respiratory tract. Avoid aerosol generation, ensure ventilation, and keep away from ignition sources when heated.

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated exposure may worsen irritation; control mists

Long-term repeated contact may lead to dermatitis in sensitive individuals; repeated inhalation of mists can sustain respiratory irritation. Use splash-resistant goggles and chemical gloves; maintain closed transfer to reduce routine exposure.

Production Process & Capacity

2-Ethylhexanol is produced mainly via oxo (hydroformylation) chemistry starting from propylene-derived intermediates, followed by hydrogenation and purification. Integration with plasticizer and acrylate units improves economics.
1 Feed Preparation
Propylene-derived streams and syngas (CO/H₂) are prepared with impurities control to protect catalysts and ensure stable oxo operation.
2 Hydroformylation (Oxo Reaction)
An olefin is hydroformylated with CO/H₂ over suitable catalysts to form aldehyde intermediates; selectivity control is crucial for the desired branched chain.
3 Hydrogenation to Alcohol
Aldehydes are hydrogenated to the corresponding alcohol (2-EH) with downstream separation to remove light ends and recycle streams.
4 Purification & Storage
Final purification by distillation delivers commercial-grade 2-EH; stored in compatible tanks with heating/blanketing where required and controls for combustible liquids.
