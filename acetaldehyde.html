Hazard Classification

Highly flammable; harmful if inhaled

Acetaldehyde is highly flammable with a flash point of -38°C. It can cause severe eye and respiratory tract irritation. Classified as a suspected carcinogen (IARC Group 2B).

Chronic Health Effects

Potential carcinogenicity

Long-term exposure may increase risk of certain cancers. Chronic irritation of respiratory system and eyes possible with repeated exposure.

Production Process & Capacity

Acetaldehyde is produced via two main routes: Wacker process (90% of global production) and ethanol oxidation (10%). The Wacker process is dominant due to lower costs and higher efficiency.
1 Feedstock Preparation
Ethylene (99.9% purity) is mixed with oxygen in a precise ratio (typically 2:1) with palladium chloride and copper chloride catalysts in aqueous solution.
2 Catalytic Reaction
Reaction occurs at 120-130°C and 3-10 bar: C₂H₄ + ½O₂ → CH₃CHO. Palladium is reduced to Pd⁰ while oxidizing ethylene, then reoxidized by Cu²⁺ which is regenerated by oxygen.
3 Product Separation
The reactor effluent is cooled and depressurized. Acetaldehyde is separated from unreacted gases through absorption and distillation at low temperatures.
4 Purification & Storage
Final purification via fractional distillation. Stored at low temperatures (-10°C) with polymerization inhibitors (hydroquinone) to prevent trimerization to paraldehyde.
