Hazard Classification

Highly flammable; very toxic by inhalation; severe irritant

Acrolein is a flammable and reactive liquid. Vapors are intensely irritating to eyes and the respiratory tract and can be very toxic if inhaled. It presents serious fire/explosion hazards and is typically handled as stabilized material.

Chronic Health Effects

Chronic respiratory/eye irritation risk

Repeated or prolonged exposure may contribute to chronic irritation/inflammation of the respiratory system and persistent eye irritation. Due to high acute toxicity, strict industrial hygiene and closed handling systems are typically required.

Production Process & Capacity

Acrolein is produced via two main routes: propylene oxidation (dominant) and glycerol dehydration (bio-based/secondary). Acrolein is commonly produced as an intermediate to acrylic acid and methionine value chains.
1 Feedstock Preparation
Propylene is purified and mixed with air/oxygen (often with steam dilution) to control temperature and selectivity in the oxidation reactor.
2 Catalytic Oxidation
Gas-phase oxidation occurs over mixed metal oxide catalysts (industrial Mo/Bi-based families) at elevated temperature. Selectivity control is critical to limit over-oxidation to CO/CO₂ and by-products.
3 Quench & Absorption
Reactor effluent is rapidly cooled (quenched) and acrolein is recovered via absorption/condensation. Unreacted gases are treated and may be recycled depending on configuration.
4 Purification & Stabilized Storage
Crude acrolein is purified (typically by distillation under controlled conditions) and stored with inhibitors/stabilizers in compatible, tightly sealed systems to mitigate polymerization and exposure risk.
