Hazard Classification

Eye damage/irritation risk; ingestion may be harmful; aquatic toxicity possible

AEO-9 (detergent-range alcohol ethoxylate) is typically classified as a nonionic surfactant with strong eye hazard. Avoid eye contact and mists; use goggles/face shield, chemical gloves, and good ventilation. Prevent release to waterways; use spill containment and proper wastewater treatment.

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated contact may cause dermatitis/dryness; aerosols can irritate the respiratory tract

Prolonged or repeated exposure to surfactant concentrates may defat skin and aggravate irritation. Maintain industrial hygiene: closed transfer where possible, eyewash/shower readiness, and training for safe handling.

Production Process & Capacity

AEO-9 is produced by ethoxylation of linear C12–C15 alcohols with ethylene oxide (EO) under controlled reaction safety and finishing/stripping.
1 Feedstock Drying & Conditioning
Detergent-range alcohol is dried (water control), filtered, and inerted; quality targets include low color/odor and controlled unsaturates.
2 Ethoxylation (EO Addition)
EO is dosed to a pressurized reactor over alkaline catalysts (typ. KOH). Heat removal, interlocks, and inerting are critical due to EO reactivity.
3 Neutralization & Finishing
Catalyst is neutralized/removed; unreacted EO and volatiles are stripped. Finishing aims to minimize by-products (e.g., 1,4-dioxane in sensitive applications) and meet color/odor specs.
4 Blending, QA & Packaging
Product is blended to target EO distribution/viscosity, then filled in drums/IBC/bulk. QA includes active content, moisture, cloud point, and residuals.
