Hazard Classification

Generally not classified as hazardous; dust may irritate eyes/airways

Bulk aluminum oxide (Al₂O₃) is chemically stable and non-flammable. Practical risks are related to fine dust generation (irritation), high-temperature operations (burn hazard), and process-specific additives. Use local exhaust ventilation, dust control, and protective eyewear; avoid breathing dust.

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated dust exposure may contribute to chronic irritation

Long-term exposure to airborne particulate (nuisance dust) may aggravate respiratory conditions in sensitive individuals. Apply industrial hygiene controls (enclosure, LEV, housekeeping) and follow applicable occupational exposure limits for respirable/total dust.

Production Process & Capacity

Alumina (Al₂O₃) is produced mainly via Bayer refining of bauxite + calcination and then tailored into activated alumina / γ-alumina supports.
1 Feedstock Preparation
Bauxite is crushed/beneficiated; caustic liquor quality is managed to control silica/impurities.
2 Digestion (Bayer)
Aluminum-bearing minerals dissolve in hot caustic liquor; insoluble residue (red mud) is separated.
3 Precipitation & Washing
Aluminum hydroxide is precipitated from the pregnant liquor, then washed to reduce soda carryover.
4 Calcination & Finishing
Hydroxide is calcined to Al₂O₃; product is classified/handled. Specialty grades may proceed to activation or shaping.
