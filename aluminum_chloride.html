Hazard Classification

Corrosive to skin/eyes; reacts with water releasing hydrogen chloride (HCl)

Aluminum chloride is a strong Lewis acid and a moisture-sensitive corrosive. Contact with water can be violent and generates acidic fumes (HCl). Use dry handling, closed transfer, local exhaust ventilation, and full PPE (chemical goggles/face shield, acid-resistant gloves, protective clothing; respiratory protection where required).

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated exposure may damage lung tissues; chronic respiratory irritation possible

Repeated inhalation exposure to corrosive aerosols/fumes can contribute to persistent respiratory irritation. Implement exposure monitoring, strict industrial hygiene, and engineering controls; keep the material dry and prevent acid-fume release.

Production Process & Capacity

Industrial AlCl3 is commonly made via direct chlorination of molten aluminum (anhydrous), with dry condensation and sealed handling.
1 Dry Utilities & Feed Preparation
Dry chlorine supply and moisture exclusion are established to prevent hydrolysis and HCl fuming during production and transfer.
2 Reaction Section
Chlorine reacts with molten aluminum to form AlCl3 vapor; heat removal and corrosion-resistant design are critical.
3 Dry Condensation & Purification
Product vapors are condensed under dry conditions and impurities are controlled (grade depends on downstream catalyst requirements).
4 Packaging / Storage (Moisture-tight)
Handled as a moisture-sensitive corrosive: sealed packaging, dry nitrogen blanketing where needed, and acid-fume controls.
