Hazard Classification

Very toxic to aquatic life with long lasting effects (H410); avoid environmental release

Anthracene is a combustible aromatic solid. Control dust, prevent ignition sources, and avoid discharge to drains/soil. Use local exhaust ventilation for dust handling and follow spill containment procedures suitable for environmentally hazardous solids.

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated dust exposure may irritate eyes/skin/respiratory tract; industrial hygiene recommended

Limit repeated exposure by using closed transfer where feasible, dedicated dust collection, and appropriate PPE (goggles, gloves, and respiratory protection when dust is present). Implement housekeeping and exposure monitoring in production and packaging.

Production Process & Capacity

Anthracene is mainly recovered from coal tar (anthracene oil fraction) and can be further purified to refined/high-purity grades.
1 Feedstock & Fractionation
Coal tar is distilled to obtain the anthracene oil range; operating conditions target PAH recovery while managing sulfur/nitrogen species.
2 Crystallization & Solid Recovery
Cooling and controlled crystallization precipitate crude anthracene-rich solids; filtration/centrifugation separates crystals from mother liquor.
3 Washing & Upgrading
Solvent washing and reprocessing reduce co-PAHs (e.g., phenanthrene) and heterocycles; product grade depends on downstream use.
4 Packaging & EHS Controls
Solid handling focuses on dust control and environmental protection; waste streams are managed to prevent aquatic impact (H410).
