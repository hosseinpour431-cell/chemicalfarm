Hazard Classification

Environmental hazard; combustible dust potential

Many SDS classify BHT as Aquatic Chronic 1 (H410: very toxic to aquatic life with long lasting effects). Avoid release to the environment. Solid dust can be combustible: minimize dust generation, keep away from ignition sources, and use standard dust-control/housekeeping practices.

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated exposure: irritation/dermatitis possible; limit occupational exposure

Prolonged or repeated contact may cause skin irritation/dermatitis in some individuals; follow good industrial hygiene (gloves, goggles, ventilation where dust may form). Use exposure monitoring and follow site SDS guidance for handling and storage.

Production Process & Capacity

BHT is typically produced via acid-catalyzed alkylation of p-cresol using isobutylene (2-methylpropene) or tert-butanol.
1 Feedstock Preparation
p-Cresol and alkylating agent are conditioned; impurities are controlled to protect catalyst performance and product color.
2 Acid-Catalyzed Alkylation
Friedel–Crafts-type alkylation forms hindered phenols; temperature control and mixing reduce by-products and oligomers.
3 Separation & Purification
Neutralization/washing and distillation (or crystallization) achieve grade specifications (technical vs food grade).
4 Packaging & Storage
Stored as solid flakes/powder/pellets with dust control; protect from heat and ignition sources; prevent environmental release.
