Hazard Classification

Combustible; harmful if inhaled/absorbed; eye irritant

2-Butoxyethanol (EGBE) is a glycol ether solvent widely used in cleaners, coatings, inks, and industrial formulations due to strong solvency and water compatibility. It is a combustible liquid; vapors/mists can irritate eyes and respiratory tract, and it can be absorbed through skin—use gloves, goggles, and ventilation, especially during spraying or heated operations.

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated exposure risk (systemic effects possible)

Repeated or high exposure (especially aerosols) may contribute to headaches, fatigue, and ongoing respiratory irritation; prolonged skin contact can cause dermatitis. Favor closed transfer, minimize mist formation, and apply occupational exposure controls in cleaning and coating operations.

Production Process & Capacity

EGBE is typically produced by etherification of ethylene oxide-derived intermediates followed by purification; it is then formulated into cleaners and industrial blends.
1 Feedstock Preparation
Ethylene oxide-derived intermediates and butanol feed are conditioned; water and impurity control support stable reaction and product quality.
2 Etherification Reaction
The glycol ether is formed through controlled etherification, where selectivity and temperature management are important to limit by-products.
3 Separation & Purification
Distillation removes light ends and unreacted alcohol; finishing steps meet water content, color, and odor specifications for industrial solvent grades.
4 Storage & Logistics
Stored as a combustible solvent in compatible tanks; shipped in drums/IBC/bulk with controls for vapor/mist exposure and ignition sources.
