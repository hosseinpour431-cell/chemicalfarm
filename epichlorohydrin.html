Hazard Classification

Flammable reactive epoxide; toxic by inhalation/skin; severe eye/skin irritation; sensitization risk

Epichlorohydrin is a volatile, flammable chloro-epoxide. Vapors can form explosive mixtures with air. It can be absorbed through skin and can cause severe irritation/burns. Use closed systems, local exhaust ventilation, grounding/bonding, and full PPE (chemical goggles/face shield, suitable gloves, and respiratory protection as required). Prevent contact with heat, ignition sources, strong acids/bases, and contaminants that can trigger polymerization.

Chronic Health Effects

Probable carcinogen (IARC Group 2A); possible mutagenicity; repeated exposure may damage respiratory tissues

Repeated or prolonged exposure may contribute to chronic irritation/inflammation of the respiratory tract and has been associated with carcinogenic risk in hazard assessments. Implement strict industrial hygiene: exposure monitoring, engineered containment, leak detection, and emergency response readiness. Follow applicable occupational exposure limits and minimize skin contact (skin absorption can lead to overexposure even when air levels appear low).

Production Process & Capacity

Epichlorohydrin is typically produced via two main routes: allyl chloride (chlorohydrin) route — petro and glycerol-to-ECH bio-route (EPICEROL® family).
1 Feedstock Preparation
Propylene/chlorine chain integration is established; allyl chloride quality and water balance are controlled for selectivity and safety.
2 Chlorohydrination to Dichlorohydrins
Allyl chloride is converted to dichlorohydrins under controlled conditions; corrosion, by-products, and heat removal are managed.
3 Dehydrochlorination to Epoxide
Base-driven ring closure produces epichlorohydrin; salt handling and reaction quench are designed for stability and EHS compliance.
4 Purification & Stabilized Storage
Distillation removes water/impurities; product is stabilized and stored in dedicated flammable/toxic service with vapor control and strict procedures.

Global Demand Analysis

ECH demand is dominated by epoxy resins used in construction coatings, composites (including wind blades), electronics encapsulation, and industrial flooring. Asia-Pacific is the largest demand hub, driven by infrastructure and manufacturing growth. Bio-route (glycerol) adoption is expanding where renewable glycerin and strict sustainability targets support the business case.

Capital Expenditure Analysis

ECH CAPEX depends heavily on chlor-alkali integration, corrosion-resistant materials, brine/salt handling, closed containment, emissions control, and high-spec distillation/stabilization systems. Bio-route projects may shift the cost profile by leveraging glycerin feedstock and alternative process steps, but still require strict toxic/flammable service design.
