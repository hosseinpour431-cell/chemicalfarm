Hazard Classification

Flammable liquid (GHS): H225 — Highly flammable liquid and vapour

EMC vapours may form flammable/explosive mixtures with air and may spread along floors. Keep away from ignition sources; use closed handling, local exhaust ventilation, grounding/bonding, and appropriate PPE.

Chronic / Repeated Exposure Notes

Repeated inhalation may cause headache, dizziness, or nausea; good ventilation is essential

Implement industrial hygiene: minimize vapour exposure, prevent static discharge, and ensure drying/low-moisture controls where battery-grade specifications apply.

Production Process & Typical Plant Scales

EMC is commonly manufactured via transesterification of dimethyl carbonate (DMC) with ethanol (often intensified using reactive distillation). An alternative route is oxidative carbonylation (less common for battery-grade due to purification demands).
1 Feed Conditioning
DMC and ethanol are dried and conditioned; water control is critical for battery-grade specifications.
2 Catalytic Conversion
Transesterification produces EMC (with methanol co-product); catalysts can be basic solids or resins, often operated in reactive distillation.
3 Separation & Purification
Fractionation removes light ends and co-products; polishing steps target very low water and ionic impurities for battery-grade EMC.
4 Storage & EHS Controls
Stored in flammable service with vapor control, inerting as required, and strict static/ignition management.
