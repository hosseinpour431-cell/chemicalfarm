Hazard Classification

Extremely flammable; toxic by inhalation; carcinogenic/mutagenic; wide explosive range

Ethylene oxide (EtO) is a highly reactive, extremely flammable gas (typically stored/handled as a pressurized liquefied gas). It can form explosive mixtures over a very wide concentration range. Use closed systems, inerting where appropriate, continuous gas detection, explosion-proof equipment, robust relief/venting, and strict ignition control. Personnel protection must include chemical eye/face protection and respiratory protection where exposure is possible.

Chronic Health Effects

Carcinogenicity & genetic effects: long-term exposure increases risk

Ethylene oxide is classified by major authorities as carcinogenic and mutagenic (and is subject to strict occupational exposure controls). Implement comprehensive industrial hygiene: engineered containment, exposure monitoring, leak management, and emergency preparedness. Avoid chronic low-level exposure in addition to preventing acute releases.

Production Process & Capacity

Ethylene oxide is produced mainly via direct oxidation of ethylene over silver-based catalysts, integrated with ethylene glycol (MEG/DEG/TEG) or EO-derivatives.
1Feedstock Purification & Mixing
Ethylene and oxygen/air are purified (trace poisons removed) and mixed with tight control to manage selectivity and safety limits.
2Catalytic Oxidation Reactor
Gas-phase oxidation over silver catalysts produces EO; reactor heat is removed and operating windows are tightly managed for selectivity and stability.
3Absorption, Stripping & Purification
EO is recovered from reactor effluent (commonly via absorption) and purified by stripping/distillation; CO2 management and by-product control are key.
4Integration, Storage & Loading
Most EO is converted on-site to MEG/EO-derivatives; any merchant EO requires dedicated pressurized systems, inhibitors/controls, vapor handling, and rigorous procedures.

Market Context
EO economics depend on ethylene feedstock, catalyst selectivity, energy integration, and downstream slate (MEG vs derivatives). Safety systems, containment, and emissions control are major cost drivers.
