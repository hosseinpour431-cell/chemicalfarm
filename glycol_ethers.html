Hazard Classification (Practical Summary)

Combustible liquids; irritation risk; some E-series have reproductive-toxicity concerns

E-series glycol ethers are widely used solvents/coupling agents. Many grades are combustible and can irritate eyes/skin; inhalation of mists/vapors should be minimized. Some E-series grades (notably EGME/EGEE) are classified for reproductive toxicity in certain regulatory systems and have restricted uses. Prefer closed handling, local exhaust ventilation, grounding/bonding, and chemical-resistant gloves + goggles/face shield; use suitable respiratory protection when required.

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated exposure may cause chronic irritation; grade-specific systemic risks exist

Long-term exposure control should consider the specific glycol ether. Maintain industrial hygiene (exposure monitoring, ventilation, and substitution where feasible). For sensitive applications, favor lower-toxicity options and ensure SDS-based risk assessment per grade.

Production Process & Capacity

E-series glycol ethers are typically produced by reacting ethylene oxide with alcohols , followed by separation and stabilization.
1 Feedstock Preparation
Alcohol feed is dried/purified; EO handling systems are verified (inerting, relief, ESD, and containment).
2 Alkoxylation Reaction
Ethylene oxide is reacted with alcohol (base/acid catalysis depending on technology) to form mono-/di- glycol ethers; temperature control is critical.
3 Separation & Purification
Unreacted alcohol/EO are recovered; fractionation/distillation sets product cut (e.g., EGBE, DEGBE) and water balance.
4 Stabilization & Logistics
Stabilizers (where required) and QA checks are applied; storage uses combustible-liquid standards with vapor control and SDS-based segregation.
