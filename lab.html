Hazard Classification

Aspiration hazard (Danger); may be harmful with skin contact

LAB is a petroleum-derived mixture used as a detergent alkylate. Avoid inhaling mists and prevent ingestion (aspiration risk). Use closed transfer where possible, local ventilation, and PPE (goggles, gloves). Keep away from ignition sources; treat as a combustible liquid.

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated skin exposure may cause dermatitis/irritation; avoid long contact

Repeated or prolonged contact can defat the skin and lead to irritation. Implement industrial hygiene, wash after handling, and use barrier PPE for routine operations. In case of accidental ingestion, do not induce vomiting—seek medical attention immediately due to aspiration hazard.

Production Process & Capacity

LAB is typically produced via two industrial routes: solid-acid (DETAL/zeolite) alkylation and HF alkylation (legacy).
1 Paraffin / Olefin Feed Preparation
C10–C13/C14 n-paraffins are recovered from kerosene cuts; dehydrogenation generates linear mono-olefins with tight selectivity control.
2 Alkylation with Benzene
Linear olefins react with benzene to form LAB in an alkylation reactor (solid-acid or HF), minimizing branched alkylate and heavies.
3 Separation & Fractionation
Unreacted benzene is recovered and recycled; LAB is fractionated to product specs while heavies are routed to HAB or fuel streams.
4 Storage, QA & Logistics
Stored as a combustible hydrocarbon liquid with vapor control and contamination prevention; quality focuses on linearity and sulfonation performance (LABSA/LAS).
