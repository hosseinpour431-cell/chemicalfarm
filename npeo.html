Hazard Classification & Handling

Harmful if swallowed / in contact with skin; eye damage & skin irritation; toxic to aquatic life (grade/SDS dependent)

Representative NP-9 SDS classifications commonly include Acute Tox. (Oral) Cat.4 (H302) and Acute Tox. (Dermal) Cat.4 (H312), with irritation/serious eye damage warnings. Use chemical goggles/face shield, gloves, protective clothing, and avoid release to drains. Implement spill containment and wastewater controls due to aquatic toxicity and persistence concerns.

Regulatory & Sustainability Notes

Restricted/phase-out in several regions; textiles & detergents are key focus areas

In the EU, Regulation (EU) 2016/26 limits nonylphenol ethoxylates in washable textile articles (0.01% by weight) with enforcement from 3 Feb 2021. In the U.S., a voluntary phase-out removed NPEs from many laundry detergent uses over the past decade, and California DTSC finalized a Priority Product regulation for laundry detergents containing NPEs (2024). These policies continue to shift demand toward alcohol ethoxylates, APG, and other alternatives.

Production Process & Capacity (Typical)

Nonylphenol ethoxylates are produced via ethoxylation of nonylphenol (NP) with ethylene oxide (EO) and are often supplied as industrial nonionic surfactant grades (e.g., NP-9).
1 Feed Preparation
Nonylphenol is dried/conditioned; EO supply and inerting (N2) are set for safe reaction control.
2 Catalytic Ethoxylation
EO is reacted with NP under basic catalysis (commonly KOH). EO:NP ratio sets the average EO number (e.g., ~9).
3 Neutralization & Finishing
Catalyst neutralization, stripping of residual EO, filtration, and quality adjustment (color, actives, water).
4 Storage, QA & EHS Controls
Bulk storage with spill containment; wastewater controls are emphasized due to aquatic toxicity and regulatory requirements.

Market reality: despite restrictions in specific consumer/textile uses, NPEs remain in some industrial applications where performance/cost is critical. Demand is increasingly shaped by compliance and substitution to alternatives.
